Album, Movies, Endorsements, Book Publications, Other Ventures

Album

In 1984, LL began his career by securing a deal with Def Jam Records, a hip-hop label. In 1985, he released his first studio album, “Radio,” debuted at number 46 on the US Billboard 200 and sold one million copies worldwide.

His breakthrough single, “I Need a Beat,” sold more than 100,000 copies. The rapper/actor has released 13 studio albums and two best hits compilations to date.

LL’s fifth studio album, ’14 Shots to the Dome,’ was a commercial success and was certified Gold by the Recording Industry Association of America. In addition, the album reached number 5 on the Billboard 200 in the United States, selling 500,000 copies.

His thirteenth album, “Exit 13,” was released as part of his final agreement with Def Jam and charted at number nine on the Billboard 200 and number three on the Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums chart.

Following his departure from Def Jam, he signed with 429 Records and released his thirteenth studio album, Authentic, in 2013.

Aside from rapping, LL Cool J has appeared in several big-budget films, including Deep Blue Sea, Toys, S.W.A.T., and The Hard Way. He’s also a producer and host of the show “Lip Sync Battle.”

Career

When LL Cool J met Rick Rubin at the age of 16, he began his musical career. Rubin was so taken with his music that the two collaborated on a record, “I Need a Beat.”

They eventually forwarded the record to Russell Simmons, artist management, who liked it as well. As a result, Simmon and Rubin started their record label, Def Jam Recordings, in the same year.

Cool J’s meteoric rise in the music industry prompted him to drop out of school and work on his debut album. ‘Radio,’ which was published in 1985 and sold over 1.5 million copies, garnered a lot of great acclaim for its dramatic lyrics.

LL Cool J continued to release moderate to major successes from 2000 through 2008 while having a successful film career.

‘Exit 13′, his last album with ‘Def Jam Recordings,’ was released in 2008. The following year, LL Cool J was cast as a regular in the CBS television series ‘NCIS: Los Angeles,’ which is still on the air today.
